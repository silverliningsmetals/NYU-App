## 3. Essay assignments and frameworks

Stern reuses the same three prompts year over year. Here's the full official text plus the framework for each.

### 3A. Short Answer — Professional Aspirations (150 words)
> *"What are your short-term career goals?"*

**The working formula** (consensus across Stacy Blackman, Accepted, Sparrey, F1GMAT):
1. Specific post-MBA role
2. 2–3 named target firms in your target industry
3. Specific function or problem you'll attack
4. One sentence on the "how" — a Stern-specific class, professor, club, or lab
5. One sentence connecting back to your background's relevance

**Buzz words to cut:** transformative leader, impact, change agent, holistic, ecosystem, drive value, leverage, synergy, paradigm shift, disrupt. Marine-specific traps: tip of the spear, mission-critical, force multiplier, kinetic, operational excellence.

**Recommended draft direction** (see Section 6 for data):
> "Post-MBA, I will join Deloitte Strategy & Operations or Kearney as a Senior Consultant in their Supply Chain Network practice, where I'll lead operational turnarounds for industrial and defense clients. Stern's Berkley Center, the Endless Frontier Labs, and Professor Foster's Supply Chain elective give me the analytical fluency to translate my $13M Marine SOF retrograde experience into private-sector operating impact. Long term, I'll return to scaling Silverlinings Metals into a mid-market industrial business — Stern's NYC industry access and the Fertitta alumni network are how I get there."

That's 134 words. Edit the named professor when you confirm one.

### 3B. Essay 1 — Change: ___ it (350 words)
> *"In today's global business environment, the only constant is change. Using NYU Stern's brand call to action, we want to know how you view change. Change: _____ it. Fill in the blank with a word of your choice. Why does this word resonate with you? How will you embrace your own personal tagline while at Stern?"*

**Personal-statement framework (per the Villanova templates your buddy shared) — maps almost perfectly to this prompt:**
- **Theme word** = "Embrace it" (you already chose it — the prompt literally asks for one)
- **Hook** = a vivid, specific personal scene that opens the theme (your "the sole reason I joined the Marines was to change-or-fail" line is your hook material)
- **Thesis & preview** = one sentence stating *why* "Embrace it" defines you + previews the two vignettes (military + civilian)
- **Body** = the two vignettes (J4 MCS COP build / Silverlinings founding / etc.), each ending with a transition back to "embrace"
- **Conclusion** = restate thesis, connect to Stern specifics (Fertitta, MVC, Berkley Center), end with imagery that callbacks to the hook

This is exactly the structure of the Villanova "paddle boarding → law" example: opening image → theme word → body experiences → forward look. Steal the architecture; keep the content yours.

**Your chosen word: "Embrace it"** — and the community evidence backs you up. Tommy Jaeger (Fertitta scholar 2023) literally wrote *"Embracing this change and choosing Stern were the two best decisions I have made since joining the military"* in his Clear Admit profile. Your instinct lines up directly with how successful Fertitta scholars have framed themselves.

**Why "Embrace it" beats your other options:**
- *"Dictate it"* and *"Conquer it"* reinforce the rigid-officer stereotype AdComm screens against
- *"Embrace it"* signals reflection, humility, growth orientation — the EQ markers Stern explicitly weights via the "IQ+EQ" framework
- Inverts the expectation for a Marine applicant in a way the reader notices

**Recommended structure (350 words):**
- **Opening (40w):** Your stated thesis from your notes — *"The sole reason I joined the Marines was to challenge myself to the point of change-or-fail. No matter the results, I was not going to be the same person."*
- **Marine vignette (110w):** One specific moment where embracing change beat resisting it. Strong candidate from your awards: the J4 MCS COP layer story — you built a tool nobody asked for because you embraced the chaos of 21-country logistics rather than just managing the inbox.
- **Civilian vignette (110w):** Either the Silverlinings Metals founding moment, your transition from active duty, or the Ashley decision. Pick the one with the cleanest "embraced change" beat.
- **Stern landing (90w):** *"How will you embrace your own personal tagline while at Stern?"* Name specifics: Fertitta summer cohort, MVC leadership, Berkley Center, the Stern Consulting Corps or Volunteer Consulting Group, NYC industry access. End with a forward-looking line.

### 3C. Essay 2 — Pick Six (visual, PDF format)
> *"Introduce yourself to the Admissions Committee and to your future classmates using six images and corresponding captions."*

**Format rules:**
- Single PDF upload (no physical, no links)
- 3-sentence intro max
- 6 images, each with a **one-sentence caption**

**Your planned 3 + recommended 3 to add:**

| # | Image | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | You + Ashley | Partnership, stability, person at home base |
| 2 | You + Marine unit | Caption it around what you **learned from them**, not what you did — AdComm has seen 10,000 platoon photos |

| **6** | **A community/mentorship moment** | Person who shaped you, a youth you coach, a Marine you mentored — visual proof of the EQ Stern weights. Bonus: tie it visually to "Embrace it" so the interviewer pulls it as the icebreaker |

**Pro move:** make one of the six images callback to your "Embrace it" word so interviewers can open with *"tell me about this Pick Six picture"* — the #1 most-reported icebreaker per Clear Admit interview reports.

## 4. EQ Endorsement plan (Col Johnson)

**Stern requires ONE EQ endorsement.** A second is optional. Yours comes from **Col Timothy W. Johnson, USA, J4 Director, CSOJTF-Central** — who signed both your JSAM and JCOMM, knows your work daily, and is the right voice.

### The five questions Stern asks the recommender:
1. Brief description of your interaction with the applicant and the applicant's role in your organization.
2. How does the applicant's performance compare to other well-qualified individuals in similar roles? Principal strengths?
3. Describe the most important piece of constructive feedback you have given the applicant. Circumstances and the applicant's response.
4. **IQ+EQ is a core value of NYU Stern**… Please provide one specific and compelling example to demonstrate the applicant's emotional intelligence.
5. (Optional) Is there anything else we should know?

Plus a rating grid on EQ-tied competencies (self-awareness, ability to receive feedback, empathy, social skills, collaboration, conflict resolution).

### What Col Johnson needs from you in a single packet:
- The five questions above + the rating grid
- Your resume (civilian-translated version)
- 3–5 specific stories he witnessed that demonstrate EQ traits (not mission accomplishment — Stern can read the awards for that)
- A one-page "civilian translation" sheet: "platoon commander" → "team lead managing 40 personnel and $X in assets"; "JCOMM" → "Joint Service Commendation Medal, awarded to fewer than X% of officers"
- A draft starter letter — see `02_EQ_Endorsement_Draft_for_Col_Johnson.md` — that he can rewrite in his voice
- Deadline, submission link from your application portal

**Critical:** Stern is screening for **EQ, not leadership/mission accomplishment.** Coach Col Johnson to lead with a story where you (a) received hard feedback and changed, (b) navigated a personnel conflict, (c) showed empathy to a junior Marine, or (d) failed and owned it. Civilian AdComms cannot parse pure tactical-proficiency narratives — they want *how you handle people*.

**Submission process:** You invite Col Johnson via the application portal; he receives a link and submits directly. Endorsements can only be accepted from the endorser — you cannot upload it for him. He can either fill the text boxes online or upload a single Word/PDF document.

Source: [Stern EQ Endorsement page](https://www.stern.nyu.edu/programs-admissions/full-time-mba/admissions/eq-endorsement)

### B. Fertitta Scholarship — tuition to $30K/year
- Merit-based, awarded at admission. No separate application.
- **Not guaranteed** — not all admitted vets get it.
- **Mutually exclusive with the Living Stipend.**

### C. Yellow Ribbon
- Stern contributes up to **$33,100/yr**; VA matches → up to **$66,200/yr** combined
- For 100% Ch33 Post-9/11 GI Bill eligible vets
- Can stack with Yellow Ribbon

### D. Living Stipend — $20K/yr (taxable)
- For vets using Ch33/Yellow Ribbon **and not already on a merit scholarship**
- Disbursed Fall + Spring
- **Mutually exclusive with the Fertitta Scholarship**

**Your aid stack decision (per your notes — agree with your assessment):**
If you have 100% GI Bill, **decline the Fertitta Scholarship and take Yellow Ribbon + Living Stipend** — that combo gives you ~$66.2K/yr tuition coverage + $20K cash stipend = $86.2K/yr support, vs. $30K tuition (~$57K/yr support) with the scholarship. Your notes captured this correctly.

**No separate Fertitta application.** Check the merit scholarship interest box; you're considered automatically as a vet admit.
